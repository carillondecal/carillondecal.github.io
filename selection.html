Berkeley Carillon Decal Selection Process

We don't believe that any of the carillon players who review applications really are justified in making a judgement on differentiating between who does/doesn't deserve a spot, so we try to keep the application review process somewhat systematic and mostly removed from personal opinion.

First, we look at repeat applicants, and provided they exhibit a baseline level of enthusiasm for what the class entails (i.e. attended a concert, talked about what they wanted to do/why they wanted to play the instrument), we accept them in order of earliest applicant to latest.

Then, if there's still space (this semester, the class was filled almost exclusively by repeat applicants), we look at first time applicants. We put them into a lottery system (which places no preference on submission time/date), then review the randomly chosen applications and make sure that they again exhibit a certain level of enthusiasm for trying to learn the instrument. This continues until the class is full.

Last semester, we had upwards of 90 applicants and only 10 spots to offer, so it really just boils down to a numbers game for first time applicants. Almost everyone who applies has what it takes to get into the class, but we sadly can't make space for them all.
